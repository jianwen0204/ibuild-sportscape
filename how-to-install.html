How to install artificial grass.

A great-looking lawn that lasts is all in the build beneath it. Here's how a proper artificial-grass installation comes together — base, drainage, joins and finish — written for Singapore's heat and monsoon rain. We design, supply and install the lot ourselves, but if you're planning a DIY job, this is the right order to work in.

Step by step

The installation, in order.

Eight stages from bare ground to a finished lawn.

01

Plan & measure

Measure the area and add about 5–10% for cuts and joins. Plan the roll direction so all the pile leans the same way, and note where water needs to fall away.

  • Measure length × width
  • Plan pile direction
  • Order a little extra
  • Check the fall for drainage
02

Clear the area

On soil, remove the existing turf and excavate roughly 75–100mm. On a hard surface like concrete or a balcony, simply clean it down — no digging needed.

  • Strip existing grass/soil
  • Or clean the hard surface
  • Remove roots & debris
03

Build the base

On ground, lay and compact a crushed-aggregate sub-base, then a layer of sharp sand or granite dust, screeded level to the fall. On slabs, balconies and rooftops, lay drainage cells instead.

  • Compact aggregate sub-base
  • Sharp sand / granite dust blinding
  • Drainage cells on hard decks
  • Grade to fall, then level
04

Membrane & edges

Lay a weed membrane to keep the base clean, and fix solid edge restraints so the lawn stays put and crisp at the borders.

  • Weed-control membrane
  • Edge restraints / timber
  • Square up the perimeter
05

Shockpad (play areas)

For playgrounds and childcare, lay a ProGame shockpad over the base — specified to the equipment's critical fall height — before the turf goes down.

  • Critical-fall-height rated
  • Lays over the base
  • Softer, safer landing
06

Roll out & settle

Roll the turf out, all pile facing one way, and let it acclimatise so it relaxes flat — important in Singapore's heat — before any cutting.

  • Pile all one direction
  • Let it relax flat
  • Avoid stretching
07

Cut & join

Trim neatly to the shape, then join lengths with jointing tape and adhesive (or hidden stitching) so the seams disappear into the pile.

  • Trim to shape
  • Jointing tape + adhesive
  • Hide the seams
08

Secure, infill & finish

Fix the edges with pins or adhesive. Add kiln-dried sand infill where needed (mainly sports), then power-brush the pile upright and rinse. Done.

  • Pin / glue the edges
  • Sand infill if required
  • Brush up & rinse

Questions

Installation questions.

Can I install artificial grass myself?
Small, simple areas can be a DIY job. For gardens, balconies, rooftops or anything needing drainage, level falls and seamless joins, professional installation protects the finish, the drainage and the warranty.
How long does installation take?
A typical residential lawn takes one to a few days depending on size, access and base work. We confirm a schedule after a site visit.
Can artificial grass be laid over concrete or tiles?
Yes — over drainage cells (or a suitable base) it works on concrete, paving, balconies and rooftops, staying level and free-draining while protecting the surface beneath.
How is drainage handled in Singapore's heavy rain?
The base is graded to fall and the turf's porous backing lets water through; on hard surfaces drainage cells carry it to the outlets — so there's no pooling for mosquitoes and the lawn is usable again within minutes.
Do I need sand infill?
For most homes and balconies, no — non-infill turf is cleaner and softer. Sand infill is mainly used on sports surfaces to support the fibres under heavy use.
